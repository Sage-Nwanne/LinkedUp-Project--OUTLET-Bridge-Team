# OUTLET Bridge Team Infrastructure Guide

## Mission

The OUTLET Bridge Team exists to help young adults move from:
- attendance,
- to participation,
- to connection,
- to community integration,
- to long-term belonging.

The team functions as the relational bridge between:
- OUTLET events,
- Connect Groups,
- leadership,
- and sustainable community development.

---

# Core Vision

The OUTLET is not simply an event ministry.

It is a relationship ecosystem.

The Bridge Team exists to:
- reduce social friction,
- help attendees feel seen,
- connect people based on shared interests,
- improve retention,
- and create leadership multiplication.

---

# Updated Pipeline Overview

## Stage 1 — Awareness
How people discover the OUTLET.

Sources:
- Instagram
- TikTok
- Flyers
- Friends
- Church announcements
- Social clips
- Campus/community outreach

Goal:
Generate curiosity and lower entry barriers.

---

## Stage 2 — Arrival
The first in-person interaction.

Goals:
- Greet attendees quickly
- Learn names
- Reduce awkwardness
- Prevent isolation
- Introduce attendees to others

Critical Success Metric:
Nobody should feel invisible.

---

## Stage 3 — Connection
The relationship formation phase.

Goals:
- Create conversations
- Identify similarities
- Introduce attendees to compatible people
- Build emotional comfort

This is the most important stage.

---

## Stage 4 — Connect Group Matching
The “bridge” phase.

The team identifies:
- interests,
- personalities,
- schedules,
- hobbies,
- spiritual goals,
- and social compatibility.

Then recommends:
- Connect Groups,
- leaders,
- environments,
- or communities.

---

## Stage 5 — Trial Integration
Equivalent to a college class drop/add period.

People should feel free to:
- try groups,
- switch groups,
- explore environments,
- and leave without guilt.

This lowers social pressure.

---

## Stage 6 — Retention
Retention means:
- recurring attendance,
- emotional safety,
- relationships,
- ownership,
- and belonging.

---

## Stage 7 — Leadership Development
Attendees eventually become:
- volunteers,
- connectors,
- facilitators,
- leaders,
- and future Connect Group leaders.

The system reproduces itself.

# Failsafes & Checks

The system must never become:
- manipulative,
- cliquish,
- impersonal,
- or performance obsessed.

---

## Human Review Layer
All major decisions require:
- human discernment,
- empathy,
- and leadership context.

---

## Multi-Person Verification
No single person should control:
- group placement,
- conflict decisions,
- or attendee assessments.

---

## Social Safety Monitoring
Watch for:
- exclusion,
- favoritism,
- burnout,
- gossip,
- unsafe leadership,
- and clique formation.

---

# Weekly Review Structure

After every event ask:
- Who returned?
- Who disappeared?
- Who was isolated?
- Which leaders excelled?
- Which environments created the best engagement?
- What friction occurred?
